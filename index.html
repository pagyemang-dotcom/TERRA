The Physics

Four Invariant Layers

G
Gravitational Potential (3D)
Earth's gravity varies with latitude, longitude, AND altitude. Every cubic metre of space has a unique gravitational potential set by the planet's mass distribution — permanent on human timescales.
W(φ,λ,h) = W₀ − g(φ,h) · H_ortho
Anomaly = W − W₀  [m²/s²]
W₀ = 62,636,853.4 m²/s² (IAG 2015)
P
Pulsar Roemer Delay (3D)
Three millisecond pulsars act as cosmic beacons. The Roemer delay depends on the observer's exact 3D position in the Solar System. Changes measurably with altitude and depth.
Δt = (n̂ · r_ECEF) / c  [milliseconds]
n̂ = unit vector to pulsar (ICRS)
r_ECEF = 3D observer position vector
I
Isotope Fingerprint
Five isotope ratios from the local medium. Each domain has unique sources. Underground rock isotopes are the most precise — geologists use them to identify strata to centimetre accuracy.
SRF: 87Sr/86Sr · δ18O · Pb/Pb · δ13C · δ15N
AIR: δ18O vapour · δD · 14C · δ13C · 222Rn
SEA: δ18O · δD · 87Sr/86Sr · δ13C · εNd
UND: 87Sr/86Sr · δ18O · Pb/Pb · δ34S · δ13C
T
Epoch Timestamp (Universal)
Fractional-year UTC anchors all four layers to a specific moment. Creates a tamper-proof chronological record. The same formula works on any planet.
T = year + (day_of_year / days_in_year)
March 20, 2026 = T[2026.215]
Resolution: microseconds

The Six Crack Types
Every mathematical problem — from IMO-level olympiad questions to real-world physics modelling — is classified into one of six crack types. The crack type determines which formulas, constraints, and solution strategies apply. AUCPID's constraint-propagation engine then reduces the problem to a single verifiable computation.
[ALG] Algebra
Symmetric Polynomials & Integer Search
Vieta's formulas, brute-force integer enumeration, equal-sum equal-product conditions. When the problem involves ages, products, roots of polynomials, or symmetric constraints.
Crack signal: "product", "age", "roots", "symmetric" Strategy: reduce to 1 variable → brute-force Verify: equal sum + product = swapped quadratic roots

[NFT] Number Theory
Legendre, LTE, Base Digits, Valuation
Legendre's formula for prime valuations in factorials. Lifting the Exponent Lemma. Base-b digit sums. Norwegian divisor triples. Trailing zeros via min(v₂, v₅).
vₚ(n!) = ∑₁₊₀₀ ⌊n/pᵏ⌋ = (n − sₚ(n)) / (p−1) Kummer: v₂(C(2m,m)) = popcount(m) = bin(m).count('1') LTE: vₚ(aⁿ−bⁿ) = vₚ(a−b) + vₚ(n)

What Is New in v14.0
3 New Crack Types
[TRG] Trigonometry via complex exponentials. [SEQ] Sequences via recurrences and generating functions. [PRB] Probability via Markov chains and linearity of expectation. AUCPID now covers all nine mathematical domains of international olympiad problems.
Dual-Phase Verification
Phase 1: algebraic verification by symbolic manipulation. Phase 2: computational verification by Python execution. Both must agree or the answer is flagged for human review. Eliminates code-output vs final-answer contradictions entirely.
Cross-Crack Detection
Some problems require two crack types sequentially — e.g. [NFT]+[FUN] or [GEO]+[ALG]. v14 detects multi-crack problems and applies both engines in sequence. Confidence scores from both passes are merged before final answer selection.
TERRA Physics Module
AUCPID v14 includes a TERRA physics sub-engine for problems involving gravitational potential, orbital mechanics, pulsar timing, or geophysical constants. The same constraint-propagation logic that solves IMO problems also computes UPA addresses from physical observations.
Adaptive Temperature
v14 adjusts sampling temperature per crack type. [NFT] and [FUN] use lower temperatures (0.3–0.5) since these problems have unique correct derivations. [CMB] and [GEO] use higher temperatures (0.6–0.9) to explore diverse geometric approaches before majority voting.
9-Type Classifier
The v14 classifier uses weighted keyword scoring across all 9 crack types simultaneously. Detects "primary" and "secondary" crack types per problem. Secondary type triggers a second refinement pass if primary confidence is below 65%.
